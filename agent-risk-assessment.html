OWASP Top 10 for Agentic Applications 2026

The OWASP Agentic Security Initiative (ASI) identifies the ten highest-impact risks for autonomous AI systems. These risks address goal hijacking, identity abuse, code execution, inter-agent communication, trust exploitation, tool misuse, supply chain vulnerabilities, memory poisoning, cascading failures, and rogue agents.

ASI-01: Agent Goal Hijack
ASI-02: Identity & Privilege Abuse
ASI-03: Unexpected Code Execution (RCE)
ASI-04: Insecure Inter-Agent Communication
ASI-05: Human-Agent Trust Exploitation
ASI-06: Tool Misuse & Exploitation
ASI-07: Agentic Supply Chain Vulnerabilities
ASI-08: Memory & Context Poisoning
ASI-09: Cascading Failures
ASI-10: Rogue Agents

EU AI EU AI Act (Regulation 2024/1689)

The EU AI Act establishes a risk-based regulatory framework with four tiers: unacceptable, high, limited, and minimal risk. High-risk AI systems must comply with Articles 9 through 15, which cover risk management, data governance, technical documentation, record-keeping, transparency, human oversight, and robustness.

Art. 5: Prohibited AI Practices
Art. 9: Risk Management System
Art. 10: Data & Data Governance
Art. 11: Technical Documentation
Art. 12: Record-keeping / Logging
Art. 13: Transparency & Information
Art. 14: Human Oversight
Art. 15: Accuracy, Robustness & Cybersecurity
Art. 51-53: GPAI Model Obligations

ISO ISO/IEC 42001:2023 Annex A Controls

ISO 42001 provides 39 controls across 9 areas (A.2 to A.10) for establishing an AI Management System (AIMS). These controls cover AI policy, internal organization, resources, impact assessment, AI lifecycle management, data management, transparency, use policies, and third-party relationships.

A.2: AI Policies
A.3: Internal Organization
A.4: Resources for AI Systems
A.5: AI System Impact Assessment
A.6: AI System Lifecycle
A.7: Data for AI Systems
A.8: Information for Interested Parties
A.9: Use of AI Systems
A.10: Third-Party & Supplier Relationships

NIST NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0)

The NIST AI RMF provides a voluntary framework organized around four core functions: Govern, Map, Measure, and Manage. Each function contains categories and subcategories that help organizations address AI risks throughout the system lifecycle in a structured, repeatable way.

GOVERN: Policies, roles, culture, legal compliance
MAP: Context, scope, risk identification
MEASURE: Analysis, metrics, tracking
MANAGE: Treat, monitor, communicate risks
